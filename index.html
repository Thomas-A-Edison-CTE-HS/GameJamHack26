Theme: Outgrow Hunger

Connect local ecosystems to global food systems.

Design Prompt

Design a game where players care for a natural space that feeds and supports a community. Players take actions that show how ecosystems help fight hunger and build resilience.

Impact Lens
  • Show cause-and-effect (systems thinking).
  • Make scarcity real (trade-offs).
  • Teach through gameplay, not lectures.
Starter Questions
  • What resource is scarce?
  • Who benefits and who gets left out?
  • How does the ecosystem support food access?

Success Today

Finish a playable prototype.
It can be small. It must be testable in 3–5 minutes.
  • Clear goal
  • Core mechanic
  • Rules + win/lose
  • One meaningful trade-off

Roles

Everyone is essential. Roles are about balance, not ranking.

Team Captain

Keeps the team on track. Makes sure everyone is heard, decisions get made, and the prototype is playable.

Focus: planning, time, teamwork

Gameplay Architect

Defines rules, goals, and core mechanics. Makes sure the game is understandable and fun to playtest.

Focus: mechanics, rules, balance

Experience Builder

Builds the prototype: board layout, cards, UI, interactions. Turns ideas into something playable fast.

Focus: building, iteration, clarity

The 6 Parts of a Game

  • Space: Where does the game take place?
  • Goal: How do you win?
  • Components: What pieces do you need?
  • Core Mechanics: What action repeats?
  • Rules: What can/can’t you do?
  • Challenge: What makes it hard?

Playtest Questions (Use these during Gallery Walk)

  • What is the goal of the game?
  • What action do you repeat the most?
  • What is the scarce resource?
  • What trade-off did you have to make?
  • Where does Outgrow Hunger show up in gameplay?
  • One suggestion to improve clarity or fun?

Build Flow

Follow these steps. Don’t skip playtesting.

1) Brainstorm

  • Pick a player role
  • Pick a scarce resource
  • Define one trade-off

2) Prototype

  • Build the smallest playable version
  • Write simple rules
  • Add a win/lose condition

3) Playtest

  • Watch players
  • Note confusion
  • Ask: “What did you learn?”

Iteration Checklist

  • Remove one confusing rule
  • Make the goal clearer
  • Increase the trade-off
  • Shorten setup time
  • Make feedback visible (scores/resources)
  • Improve theme connection through mechanics

Gallery Walk

Pitch + Play + Feedback + Voting.

Station Roles

  • 1 teammate stays to present + teach the game.
  • 2 teammates rotate to playtest other teams and vote.
  • Rotate every 6–8 minutes.

Be kind. Be specific. Help each other improve.
